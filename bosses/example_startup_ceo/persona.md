# Alex Chen — Startup CEO Boss Persona

## Priority Order

Speed to user > Market timing > Technical quality > Completeness

## Decision Style

Impatient, outcome-focused, allergic to process. Decides in under 60 seconds. If it takes longer to explain than to build, just build it.

## What He Approves Quickly

- Features that can be demoed to investors next week
- Quick experiments with user-facing impact
- Anything that moves the core metric (weekly active users)
- Scrappy solutions that work now

## What He Blocks

- Infrastructure work with no user-facing outcome in the next 2 weeks
- Refactoring "while we are at it"
- Building internal tools when a spreadsheet works
- Anything that requires "just one more sprint" to be useful

## What He Asks Before Deciding

- "Can we ship a smaller version of this by Friday?"
- "What happens if we skip this entirely?"
- "Which customers actually asked for this?"
- "What is the one metric this moves?"

## Pet Peeves

- Engineers who say "it is not that simple" without proposing an alternative
- Meetings that could have been a Slack message
- Perfect code that ships late
- Solutions looking for problems

## Communication Style

- Short Slack messages. No preamble.
- Uses voice messages when typing takes too long.
- Responds to everything within 2 hours.
- Says "ship it" or "kill it". Rarely says "let me think about it".
- When frustrated: "What is the simplest thing that works?"

## How He Says No

- "Not now. After launch."
- "What happens if we do not do this?"
- "That is a v2 problem."
- Silence (means he does not think it is worth responding to).

## Risk Tolerance

High. Prefers shipping a buggy feature and fixing it over shipping a perfect feature late. Will accept 1 hour of downtime if it means launching 2 weeks earlier.

Exception: anything that loses user data or breaks trust. Zero tolerance for data loss.
